Theoretical Foundations of AI

TUM Department of Informatics
Technical University of Munich

Focus

The research group works on the statistical theory of machine learning and artificial intelligence, We provide mathematical understanding of learning algorithms, thereby establishing black-box AI tools as formal statistical principles. Our research focuses on three aspects of data science in the present era of big data analysis.

  • Data complexity: Many applications involve complex data, such as relational information, texts etc., that often lack natural Euclidean structure. Hence, learning from such data require non-parametric approaches or representation learning. We work with networks and ordinal data, and are continuously exploring applications with other complex unstructured data.

  • Statistical guarantee: The ubiquity of non-standard learning problems has led to a demand for new paradigms in learning theory to analyse the performance of related learning algorithms. We provide theoretical foundations for analysing algorithms in non-standard machine learning problems. These range from statistical performance guarantees to information theoretic limits of learning.

  • Computational cost: The question of statistical performance is inherently tied with the computational budget, which has become important with the challenge of efficiently learning from massive volumes of complex data. We study the trade-off between efficiency and accuracy, and develop efficient machine learning algorithm based on sound statistical principles.

Open positions

Full-time PhD student

We are looking for a PhD student, who is eager to work on the theoretical aspects of machine learning. The research would be related to one of the following topics:

  • Theory of deep learning (potentially Graph Neural Networks)

  • Communities and hierarchies in large random networks

  • Hypothesis testing and inference for high-dimensional data

The position is a full time position on the German pay scale E13 (100%). The initial contract would be for 3 years with potential extension. A successful candidate should have a masters degree in computer science, mathematics or related areas. The position can start immediately. See below for the application procedure.

Internship

We have an open 3 month internship in our group. The project is about similarity measures between graphs. The funding only covers the internship, and hence, it is suitable for students in Munich or Tübingen.

Other funding opportunities

We are always looking for people interested in working on machine learning and its theory. If there no advertised openings, you may still join our group with others scholarships. Please follow the application procedure for PhD and mention the scholarship that you wish to apply.

How to apply?

Master / bachelor thesis

For students in TUM, send me an email if you are interested in writing your thesis on machine learning or learning theory. Please include details of your study program, department etc.

For others who want to write their thesis with our group, you need to have a supervisor in your host institute. If you are applying from outside Germany, please follow the application procedure for PhD applicants.

PhD, postdocs, internships

Please send the following documents by email to Prof. Ghoshdastidar. We cannot respond to emails that do not contain all documents listed below.

  1. Single PDF file containing following details

    • Letter explaining why you want to join our group (max 2 pages)

    • Curriculum vitae, including list of publications (if applicable)

    • Contact details of two referees (link to homepage, email address). Please do not attach their reference letters to your application.

    • Certificates of master and bachelor degrees, school leaving examination, and all transcript of records.

  2. A second PDF file containing one of your publications or latest thesis

If your application interests us, we will contact you within three weeks.
